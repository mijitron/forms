Why Responsive Web Design Matters

Responsive Web Design or RWD matters today because the way consumers retrieve and interpret content has immensely changed from the last decade. People of all ages, diversities and generations can now access the web directly through smartphones, tablets, desktop computers, smart tvs, video game consoles and trust me there is much more to come before the end of the century.

With the number of internet-capable devices increasing, the need for RWD has become an industry standard for all e-commerce sites. According to an article posted by the Interactive Media in Retail Group (IMRG), “…online retail sales made through mobile devices (smartphones and tablets) exceed[ed] 50% for the first time in Q4 2015/2106.”

As consumers become accustomed to accessing information a certain way, it has become the UX/UI designer’s job to ensure that these consumers are getting the best user experience. This experience should be the same regardless of the device screen’s size which is why RWD is a fundamental concept for any aspiring web designer.
